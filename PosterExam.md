Coursework MS Final Poster Exam
Completed in the semester you intend to finish your MS degree requirements and graduate

About the exam	
•	You can expect the first informational email about the process 2-3 months
before the final day of classes for the term in which you will graduate.
•	The exam usually takes place 2-4 weeks before the end of Spring final exams and 1-2 weeks before the end of Fall final exams.
•	The location of the exam varies depending on the number of graduates and availability of venues
•	The exam is open to the public

Before the exam	
Application for graduation
•	Apply to graduate at the beginning of your final semester
•	Use the apply to graduate function in DukeHub

Final Coursework MS Program of Study
•	Find it at Canvas>Modules>MS Program> ECE MS Degree Requirements and Courses>ECE Program of Study MS
o	Choose the correct form for the year you began the program
•	Submit the form to the Master’s Coordinator (Krista Turner) at ecemastersteam@duke.edu. NOTE: For your submission at the beginning of your final semester, no advisor signature or final course grades are required.  Committee Formation
•	The Masters Team will create your final exam committee and submit your required committee approval form to the Graduate School.
•	Note: The committee members  -- whose names will eventually appear in the “advisor” section of your DukeHub account – are not the professors who will attend your presentation. (For more details, see “The poster exam session,” below.)

Content	
Exam Requirement
The basic requirement of the exam is a poster presentation (approximately 5 minutes in length) that describes a project you undertook for an ECE graduate course. Most presentations include the following:
•	Hypothesis/research question
•	Data
•	Discussion of the data
•	Conclusion
 
NOTE: There is no required format for your poster. You can get some format ideas by viewing the PhD Posters website listed below.

The Poster
•	You are responsible for
o	creating your poster
	The trifold poster boards provided by the Masters Team measure 3’ (height) by 4’ (width including all 3 panels)
o	having the poster printed
	You may want to use PhD Posters (phdposters.com), which has a next-day Duke campus pick-up option
o	paying for the printing
o	bringing the poster to the examination location
	Easels, trifold poster boards, and poster clips will be provided
•	If you choose to base your poster on a group project, adhere to the following guidelines when you create your poster:
o	credit your collaborator(s)
o	focus the poster specifically on your own contribution to the group project. Don’t choose a project for which you had many (3+) collaborators.
•	A poster may focus on unfinished research with the following guidelines:
o	clearly articulate the intended goal of the project
o	thoroughly explain the research that has been accomplished thus far
o	explain what might have been done with more time or resources

The Presentation
•	Your presentation will be reviewed by two or more ECE faculty members 
•	Your presentation should last about 5 minutes
o	Remember that you will present the poster at least two times
•	If your presentation is based on a group project, clearly state this fact at the beginning of your presentation and focus the bulk of your presentation on your own contributions.

The poster exam session	
Student responsibilities
•	Arrive at the exam at the requested time to set up your easel (supplied) and exam poster.
•	Check in with the Grad Team and receive any day-of-exam instructions.
•	At least two ECE professors will attend your presentation. Their arrival time is a matter of chance, depending on their availability. These professors will each give you a P/F grade. The grades will then be reported to the Committee.
•	You will be notified by ECE Masters Team staff once your exam has been reviewed by the Committee, and you will be given further instructions at that time. 
•	You will need to stay at your assigned position until you receive instructions from the Masters Team. Come prepared to stay for the entire listed time.
•	Once you have received permission to leave, break down your poster station and return materials to the check-in table.
o	You may keep your poster
